Using a Salifert Alkalinity Testing Kit and CRS


How are you treating your water?


This test assumes you have a Salifert Alkalinity (Kg/Alk) Testing kit. If you don't have one, these can be purchased for around £10 from a pet store, or online. It also assumes you have some acid called "CRS" or now called "AMS" which is a carbonate reducing solution. This can be purchased online or from any home brew shop for less than £5.



I'm treating all my water together (strike and sparge water all in one bucket)

Note: Your MEQ/L reading is on the far right column of the sheet that comes with your Salifert kit. This is not your CaCO3 reading. If you only have a CaCO3 reading, simply divide this by 50 to get your MEQ/L reading - e.g. 100 CaCO3 / 50 = 2 MEQ/L

By your alkalinity figure it looks like your calcium is below 50ppm. Please consider...
• For a hoppy beer add a teaspoon of gypsum per 23 litres of beer. Half in the strike water and half directly in the boil.
• For a malty beer add a teaspoon of calcium chloride per 23 litres of beer. Half in the strike water and half directly in the boil.
Calcium is useful for yeast health to ensure your beer ferments properly and settles properly after fermentation.
